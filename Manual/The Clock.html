On the Subject of The Clock

Tick-tock... as if you needed more reminding that time is running out.

  • Use the dials to the right of the clock to adjust the hands on the clock.
  • Set the clock to the correct time and press the button between the dials to disarm the module.

Step A: Determine Starting Time

Use the circular chart on Page 2 to simulate a clock face for the appropriate hand. The resulting positions of each hand will provide a starting time needed to complete the module.

  • For minutes, determine a straight line on the chart that travels through all appropriate categories matching the features of the clock on the module, without intersecting any other categories.
  • Determine a second line for hours by applying the deviation adjustment described on Page 2 to each ring in the chart, one at a time in order, and then round the result down to full hours.

Step B: Determine Final Time

Follow these steps using the starting time determined in Step A. Remember to adjust to the appropriate hour if the minutes are ever 60 or greater before continuing to the next step.

  1. In minutes, add the sum of all digits in the serial number.
  2. If the clock face is black and the number of minutes is now between one and twelve, swap the minutes and hours.
  3. If the color of the hour and minute hands does not match that of the numerals, add 1 hour and 23 minutes.
  4. If the bomb has registered any strikes, multiply the number of strikes by the number of D-Cell batteries present and add that many hours. Repeat this process using AA-Cell batteries for minutes.
  5. If the casing and dials on the clock are a color other than gold, swap the digits in the minutes place (considering it as having a leading zero if necessary).
  6. Subtract one hour for each minute remaining on the digital countdown timer, ignoring seconds.
